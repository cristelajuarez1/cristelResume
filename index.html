I am Marlene Juarez, a Data Analyst and Developer with a holistic perspective that combines analytical thinking, intuition, and creativity.

The Person Behind the Data

I see myself as someone who balances introspection with openness, able to adapt to different environments with a flexible mindset—even in complex or chaotic situations. I enjoy questioning the status quo, thinking beyond conventional frameworks, and finding new ways to interpret reality. This natural curiosity, along with my interest in exploring different perspectives, has allowed me to develop a strategic, critical, and deeply human way of thinking.


Beyond technology—which is my foundation—I am a self-taught, multidisciplinary learner. I’m interested in fields such as economics, philosophy, psychology, and astronomy, which strengthens my analytical capabilities and allows me to understand not only systems, but also behaviors, structures, and broader contexts.


I am naturally driven to understand the “why” behind things: why systems behave the way they do, why people make certain decisions, and how patterns influence outcomes. This mindset enables me to analyze data, processes, and business challenges with depth, intention, and critical thinking.


Throughout my more than five years of experience in the technology sector—particularly within the telecommunications industry—I’ve identified that one of the main causes of organizational chaos is poor or ineffective communication. I have also witnessed complex dynamics where misinformation, manipulation, and unethical practices create unfair environments, impacting both people and results.


These experiences led me to develop a clear approach: not just solving problems at the surface level, but identifying and addressing root causes. This is how Eterwebstudio was created—a space focused on analyzing, structuring, and optimizing processes, with the goal of reducing organizational noise and bringing order to complexity.


My work focuses on identifying the true causes of problems, whether by establishing proper controls or eliminating practices that no longer add value. I’ve observed that many organizational dysfunctions stem from the misuse of power or from structures that have failed to evolve.


My approach is centered on building environments where each person operates in a role aligned with their strengths—without the need to manipulate systems or create imbalance. I aim to foster a culture of genuine collaboration, where both leaders and teams develop a deeper understanding of their impact and work together, rather than in silos.


I believe in more conscious organizations—where order is not imposed, but built through clarity, communication, and shared responsibility.
